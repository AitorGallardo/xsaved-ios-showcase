# Product

## The problem

Most people save tweets on their phone, in the moment — and then never find them again. The native bookmarks screen is a single endless list: no search, no grouping, no sense of what's in there. The saving works; the remembering doesn't.

## The idea

Turn that list into a library you'd actually open. Image-forward, searchable, and quick to add to from anywhere on the phone — without feeling like a heavier, slower version of the thing it's fixing.

## What it does

- A scrollable grid of everything you've saved, led by the media; text-only tweets become clean quote cards.
- Instant search across the whole library.
- A detail view with full media, your notes, and tags.
- Saving from the system share sheet, voice, and search; a home-screen widget; and live sync with the rest of your devices.

## Principles

**It should feel like it belongs on the phone.** The bar is a first-party app, not a wrapper around a website. That shapes nearly every decision.

**Lead with the image.** A wall of text is what the native screen already is. Cards are mostly picture, with a short caption — so the grid reads like a board of everything you've kept.

**Be present, not loud.** Saving shouldn't mean opening the app. It lives in the share sheet, in search, and on the home screen, and otherwise stays quiet.

**Stay a reader.** The phone doesn't do the heavy collecting itself; it reads an already-clean library and stays fast and light as a result.
